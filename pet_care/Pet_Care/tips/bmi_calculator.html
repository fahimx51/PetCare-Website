Pet Body Condition Scoring (BCS)

Start measuring your pet’s health

  1. Determine your pet’s body condition with a Body Condition Score(BCS). See your veterinarian or start with an at-home body check.
  2. Weigh your pet and track weight. How does it compare over time?
  3. Calculate daily caloric needs. Do you know how many calories your pet needs a day?
  4. Measure or weigh your pet’s food and treats. How many calories and servings are you actually giving your pet?

How to Assess Body Condition Score

The Body Condition Score is typically rated on a scale from 1 to 9, with 1 being underweight, 5 being ideal, and 9 being overweight. Here's a general guide for assessing BCS:

For Dogs:

  • Underweight (1-3): Ribs, spine, and pelvic bones are easily visible. No palpable fat, and an obvious waist and abdominal tuck.
  • Ideal/Normal (4-5): Ribs are easily felt but not visible. A visible waist and abdominal tuck.
  • Overweight (6-9): Ribs are difficult to feel, and there's noticeable fat covering them. The waist is less defined, and there may be a sagging abdominal area.

For Cats:

  • Underweight (1-3): Ribs, spine, and pelvic bones are easily visible. No palpable fat, and an obvious waist.
  • Ideal/Normal (4-5): Ribs are easily felt but not visible. A visible waist and abdominal tuck.
  • Overweight (6-9): Ribs are difficult to feel, and there's noticeable fat covering them. The waist is less defined, and there may be a sagging abdominal area.
